# Design System: JustBuild
**Project ID:** Local Next.js project (`/Users/shadman/Documents/justbuild`)

## 1. Visual Theme & Atmosphere
The interface now follows a cinematic retro-futurist scroll-story direction. The mood is darker, more theatrical, and more spatial than a standard landing page. The page should feel like drifting through a floating command environment in deep space: layered panels, a glowing central relic, and chapter beats revealed through scroll. The design is intentionally more immersive than flat. Motion is part of the storytelling, not just decoration.

## 2. Color Palette & Roles
- **Deep Vacuum Black (`#05060A`)**: Used for the page foundation and the deepest background shadows. It creates the sense of infinite depth.
- **Cold Space Navy (`#090B12`)**: Used in the vertical gradient transitions through the page. This softens the pure black foundation and supports atmospheric depth.
- **Electric Command Cyan (`#00F0FF`)**: Used for command emphasis, story highlights, and luminous glows around the core object. This remains the primary active accent.
- **Nebula Violet (`#B347FF`)**: Used for secondary glows, dimensional blend fields, and depth contrast in the story atmosphere.
- **Starlight White (`rgba(255,255,255,0.98)`)**: Used for stars, bright focal highlights, and the sharpest visual accents.
- **Glass Mist White (`rgba(255,255,255,0.03 - 0.08)`)**: Used for floating panels and chapter cards. It creates translucent depth without flattening the scene.

## 3. Typography Rules
**Space Grotesk** remains the display voice. It should be used in oversized, close-tracked headlines with strong vertical compression to support the cinematic scale of the hero. **Manrope** carries all narrative copy and supporting explanation with a smoother, calmer rhythm. **Geist Mono** is reserved for commands, chapter indices, and UI telemetry language. Type hierarchy should be bold and sparse: fewer words, larger moments, tighter lines.

## 4. Component Stylings
* **Story Panels:** Large floating glass slabs with generously rounded corners, stronger blur, and deep shadow stacks. They should feel like suspended UI fragments in 3D space.
* **Core Object:** The skull image is treated as a central relic. It sits inside a circular shell with glow halo and ring structures, serving as the anchor for the scroll narrative.
* **Chapter Cards:** These should feel like quieter descendants of the floating story panels: rounded, luminous, and slightly elevated, but less dramatic than the hero-stage layers.
* **Commands:** Monospace command text should glow subtly in cyan and read like active system instructions rather than flat labels.

## 5. Layout Principles
The page is organized as a scroll story with a long-height hero and a sticky stage. The hero should pin in place while layered objects shift in depth and angle as the user scrolls. Later sections should feel like chapter aftermath rather than unrelated marketing blocks. The layout uses more vertical narrative tension, more overlap, and more central composition than the earlier card-grid approach.

## 6. Depth, Motion & Background Rules
- The background should read as spatial, not merely atmospheric.
- Stars must be visible enough to register at a glance, but not so dense that they become visual noise.
- The sticky hero stage should create the sense of layered depth through perspective and independent panel motion.
- Cyan and violet glows should behave like volumetric light fields rather than flat circles.
- Scroll motion should reveal structure and sequence: entry, transformation, and arrival.
